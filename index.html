Abstract

      People love to complain about how the trians are always late, never on time, cancelled, however, there are lots of aspects which can impact the "perfect case scenario" timetable that the Train planners for SWR produce. 
      We'll be taking a look at SWRs main "trunk route", the SWML between London Waterloo, through to Working and onto the Portsmouth Direct Line (PDL)

Introduction

      SWR is a Train Operating Company (TOC), who is owned by the FirstGroup and the MTR Corporation. With operating a mix of commuter, semi-fast and fast services, in order to aid people getting from the busy capital from Waterloo and travel as far as Weymouth in 
      Dorset. But in this article, we’ll be focussing on the SWML from Waterloo to Woking and then the PDL to Guildford which leaves the SWML at Woking, hooks around past Worplesdon and arrives at Guildford. (‘South Western Railway’, 2024; ‘South Western Main Line’, 
      2024; ‘Portsmouth Direct Line’, 2024)

      There are 3 core aspects when it comes to timetabling, timetabling (timing), roster diagrams and rolling stock diagrams. Each of these have you could say own subset among them, but that’s a bit too complicated, we’ll be mainly looking at the surface of the timing 
      section and what they do to make the timetable as optimal as possible.
